Financial Basics

Budgeting

Budgeting is the cornerstone of effective financial management. Start by listing all sources of income, followed by expenses, categorized as essentials (like housing, transportation) and discretionary (like dining out, entertainment).

Saving

Building wealth begins with disciplined saving. Aim to allocate a set percentage of your income to a high-yield savings account. Consider creating a diversified portfolio of saving goals: short-term for luxuries, long-term for life aspirations.

Spending Wisely

Understanding the difference between needs and wants is the key to spending wisely. Needs are essentials, like food, shelter, and transportation. Wants are things you enjoy but could live without, like dining out or entertainment.

Earning Money

Earning your own money gives you freedom and independence. You may have options like part-time jobs, freelancing, or small businesses (like tutoring or selling crafts). Consider what you enjoy and what skills you have.

Investing

Investing allows you to grow your wealth by putting your money to work. Unlike saving, which keeps your money safe in a bank, investing involves some risk in exchange for potential growth.
